Section 2

Audience Hierarchy

Ranked by priority. When design or content decisions conflict between audiences, the higher-ranked audience wins.

Priority 1

Donors — Current and Prospective

Who they are: Individuals motivated by the pro-life mission, activated by stories of lives saved, injustice (lawsuits, attacks on pregnancy centers), and tangible impact (APRN, Option Line). Arrive via email campaigns, direct mail, Google searches related to lawsuits or news, and referrals.

Job-to-be-done: Understand what Heartbeat does and feel confident their donation creates direct, measurable impact. Move from awareness to conviction to giving.

What they need to feel: "This organization is doing something real. My money goes directly to saving lives. I can see the proof."

Desired action: Donate. Secondary: subscribe to nurture list if not ready to give yet.

Priority 2

General Public — Mission Learners

Who they are: People who've heard about Heartbeat through news, a friend, a church, or a search query. Curious but not yet committed. May or may not have a personal connection to the pregnancy help movement.

Job-to-be-done: Understand what Heartbeat International is, what pregnancy help means, and how the organization operates — credibly and clearly.

What they need to feel: "This is a trustworthy, compassionate, and effective organization. I understand what they do and I respect it."

Desired action: Subscribe to learn more (soft conversion to nurture list). Secondary: find a local pregnancy help center (directory).

Priority 3

Pastors and Church Leaders

Who they are: Church leaders exploring how to connect their congregation to the pregnancy help mission. May be looking for resources, partnership opportunities, or ways to support local centers.

Job-to-be-done: Find specific resources for integrating pregnancy help into their church's ministry. Understand how Heartbeat can equip them.

What they need to feel: "This organization will help me lead my church toward meaningful action, not just awareness."

Desired action: Engage with pastor-specific resources. Secondary: connect with Heartbeat for partnership.

Audience not served by this site: Network affiliates (pregnancy centers, maternity homes, adoption agencies). They belong on heartbeatservices.org. Currently, some affiliates land on the international site by accident. The new architecture must include clear redirection for affiliates who arrive at the wrong site.

Section 4

Content Strategy

Content That Must Exist at Launch

Organizational overview and mission. Program pages (Option Line, APRN, equipping the movement). Stories of Life Saved (migrate existing stories — these are actively used across channels). Worldwide Directory (rebuilt on HubSpot). Financial transparency / annual report data (legally required). Donation flow (Virtuous embed). Lawsuit / defending the movement content.

Content That Can Phase In Post-Launch

Pastor/church resources (page exists at launch, content depth grows over time). Expanded newsroom. Downloadable resources for donor acquisition (inbound marketing documents Andrea mentioned — "give us your email and download this"). Blog or thought leadership content (if deemed valuable after AI search strategy assessment).

Content Sources

HBI provides: stories, program descriptions, financial data, legal updates, brand-approved photography and video. JDA creates: site copy (grounded in brand guide), page architecture, UX writing, conversion-optimized donor journey copy. Repurposed: annual report and case study content (Andrea is sending these — they "drive the messaging" and illustrate the integrated program system).

AI Search and Schema Strategy

Joe flagged this in the session: AI search is reducing website traffic across all clients because answers appear in summaries. The new site must be architected for schema markup so Heartbeat's content surfaces as authoritative answers — not just as a link in results. With 10,000+ pages currently, crawlers only index a fraction. The dramatically reduced page count (12-15 pages) means every page gets fully indexed and every piece of content earns its place in search results.

Section 5

Technical Requirements

Platform

Complete departure from Joomla. New CMS — specific platform recommendation to come from JDA development team. Must support: preview before publish (currently impossible on Joomla — a stated pain point), role-based editorial workflow, HubSpot integration, Virtuous donation embed, and Moodle authentication bridge for Academy.

Security

The current site is under active bot attack — 150,000 attempts in 15 minutes during a call with IT. The Joomla framework is too outdated for modern security features. The new platform must have built-in bot protection, DDoS mitigation, and security features that don't require Cloudflare workarounds. Separating the two sites eliminates the cascade failure pattern where an attack on one site takes down both.

Integrations

HubSpot: Replacing CiviCRM. Powers the Worldwide Directory search, contact management, and conversion tracking. Therese is leading the implementation.
Virtuous: Donation processing. Embedded on-site. Donor journey tracking from landing page through donation completion.
Moodle: Heartbeat Academy. Stays as-is. Bridge connection for user authentication must be maintained.
Campaign Monitor: Current email nurture list tool. Subscribe forms on the new site feed into Campaign Monitor.

Analytics and Measurement

JDA is building a measurement plan that tracks: primary conversions (donations), pre-conversion actions (subscribe, time on key pages, pages per session), and engagement signals that indicate a visitor is likely to convert. The current analytics are compromised by bot traffic — accurate measurement requires the new platform's security layer to be in place first. Andrea specifically requested the ability to follow a donor's full journey: "This is exactly where they went. This is how they got to this page."

Section 6

Copy Seeds

Directional copy for major sections. Grounded in the brand guide and discovery. Specific enough for a copywriter to react to — not final, not lorem ipsum.

Homepage Hero
Every life reached is a life changed.
Tone: warm authority. Not pleading — confident. Heartbeat has been doing this since 1971. Over 4,000 affiliates. 2 million connections through Option Line in 2025 alone. Lead with scale and impact, not with ask. The donation CTA earns its place by proving the mission first.
Option Line
2,000 calls a day. Every one of them matters.
Tone: urgency with warmth. The 24/7 reality — someone is answering the phone right now. Bilingual. Personal. The numbers are staggering (2M+ connections) but each one is a real person. Balance the statistical scale with individual story intimacy. This is where "your donation does something right now" becomes tangible.
APRN
8,000 second chances. And counting.
Tone: hope against the clock. Time is of the essence — this is a rescue network. 1,500+ healthcare professionals ready. The emotional hook is the mother who changed her mind and got a second chance. The donor hook is that every dollar directly enables that rescue.
Defending the Movement
They came for pregnancy centers. We stood up.
Tone: resolute, not defensive. The California and New York AG lawsuits. The vandalism in 2022. This is the David vs. Goliath content that activates justice-motivated donors. Frame Heartbeat as the organization that represents and defends the entire movement — not a victim, but a protector.
Donate CTA
Your gift saves lives today. Not someday. Today.
Tone: direct, concrete. No abstract "support our mission." Every donation connects to a specific, immediate impact: an Option Line call answered, an abortion pill reversal completed, a pregnancy center equipped. The word "today" is intentional — it mirrors the 24/7 reality of what Heartbeat does.
