The Contractor Math

We currently spend $70k+ annually on a single external contractor. That contractor cannot be retooled — we won't provide AI training or tools to an external resource whose rate stays fixed regardless. As our internal AI capabilities accelerate, every dollar spent on legacy-rate contracting becomes less efficient relative to what an AI-activated internal developer can produce.

Filling the open Senior AI Engineer position is not a comparable or lower cost — it's roughly double. We're looking at $100–125k salary with full benefits (e.g. $110k + 17% benefits = ~$128.7k fully loaded). The delta vs. the contractor is the real add: $110k + 17% − $70k = ~$59k incremental cost. The case isn't cost savings; it's strategic: a full-time, AI-native resource whose output scales with the tooling and who activates the entire agency — not a fixed-rate external whose value is declining. The contractor spend isn't eliminated overnight, but the trajectory is clear: internal AI-native talent replaces external legacy labor as the retainer pipeline grows.

The technical debt of dozens of legacy client sites is real and it's not going away. Asking the Director and Lead Developer to simultaneously maintain that portfolio, adopt entirely new AI-native workflows, and deliver retainer-grade output at speed is a path to burnout and slow execution. The Senior AI Engineer separates the missions: legacy maintenance stays covered, and the agency gets an AI activation resource who makes everyone faster — not just a third developer.
