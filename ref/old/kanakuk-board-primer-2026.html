Crisis Briefing

What the evidence shows, what it means, and what the board must decide.
March 2026 Pre-Call Leadership Review JDA Worldwide
Situation

Where Kanakuk Stands Today

  • 01 Legal aggression underway. Two cease-and-desist letters sent to Shawn Ryan. Ryan's attorney rejected both, publicly invited discovery, and framed the action as intimidation of a victim's family member.
  • 02 Leadership unchanged. Joe White, who led Kanakuk through the Pete Newman era, remains CEO. The White family controls multiple board seats. The organizational structure in place during the abuse period is functionally intact.
  • 03 Contradictory public record. White's 2022 Open Letter stated opposition to any legal action meant to suppress victims' voices. The 2026 C&D letters threaten legal action against people telling those stories.
  • 04 NDA ground is collapsing. Trey's Law (Texas, June 2025) voids NDAs in sexual abuse settlements retroactively — named after a Kanakuk victim. Missouri's equivalent took effect August 2025. Five additional states have pending legislation.

"The legal and cultural presumption has shifted. The burden is now on Kanakuk to prove its confidentiality practices were not about hiding abuse patterns."

Critical vulnerability: The cease-and-desist letters characterize critics as acting with "callous and malicious disregard of the truth" — the identical language used by the SBC when dismissing survivors as "opportunistic." That strategy failed catastrophically.

Comparative Analysis

Six Institutions, One Pattern

Penn State · 2011
Substantially Recovered
Board acted in four days — fired coach and president, hired independent investigator (Freeh Report), embraced findings publicly. Speed and completeness of leadership change enabled recovery.
Michigan State / USA Gymnastics · 2015–18
Permanently Damaged
Delayed action. McKayla Maroney NDA argued as "requested by the victim" — identical to Kanakuk's current framing. Public rejected the distinction. Four CEOs in 23 months. ~$1B in total settlements.
Baylor University · 2015–16
Rebuilt Under New Leadership
Board fired coach and president even though Starr claimed personal ignorance. Standard was institutional accountability, not criminal culpability. Embraced 105 reforms. Rebuilt reputation under new leadership.
Archdiocese of Los Angeles · 2002–2024
Trust Permanently Impaired
Initial secrecy and NDAs deepened public outrage. $1.5B in total settlements — staggeringly higher than if transparency had come earlier. Demonstrates the compounding cost of delay.
Southern Baptist Convention · 2019–22
Closest Kanakuk Analogue
Faith institution that framed accountability as an attack on the ministry. Called survivors "opportunistic." Maintained secret list of 700+ abusers. DOJ investigation opened. 1.5M members lost.
Boy Scouts of America · 2010–23
Near-Fatal
Maintained internal "Perversion Files" for decades. Delayed action until forced by 82,000 claims. $2.46B settlement. Membership fell 60%. Demonstrates what waiting costs measured in decades.
Synthesis

What the Evidence Tells Us

Failed in every case
  • ✕Legal aggression against accusers. C&D letters, defamation threats, adversarial litigation universally backfired — amplified coverage, alienated sympathizers, opened devastating discovery.
  • ✕Framing accountability as an attack on the mission. Every institution that characterized critics as enemies suffered the longest crises.
  • ✕Retaining implicated leadership. Leaders in charge during the abuse period made genuine reform impossible in the eyes of every stakeholder group, regardless of personal culpability.
  • ✕Using NDAs to manage the narrative. Every NDA became a symbol of silencing. The legal tools are now being voided retroactively by state legislatures.
  • ✕Delay. Penn State acted in days. The BSA waited decades and paid $2.46B. Every month of inaction increased the eventual cost.
Worked in every case
  • ✓Leadership transition. New leaders, unconnected to the crisis, could acknowledge failures, commission genuine investigations, and center victims in ways incumbents could not.
  • ✓Truly independent investigation. Freeh Report, Pepper Hamilton, Guidepost — credible because they were independent and because leadership accepted findings publicly.
  • ✓Victim-centered communication. Specific apologies paired with concrete structural action. Empty statements without change were consistently rejected.
  • ✓Comprehensive institutional reform. New safety protocols, independent oversight boards, mandatory training, transparent reporting — demonstrated commitment beyond statements.
Unambiguous Finding

There is no precedent in which an institution successfully navigated a crisis of this nature while retaining the leadership in place during the abuse.

Recommended Path

Three Phases, Clear Sequence

01
Leadership Transition
Immediate
  • Joe White announces transition from CEO, framed around his health and the ministry's centennial
  • White family members step down from the Board to enable independent governance
  • Interim leader with no connection to the Newman era appointed immediately
  • National search for permanent leadership announced using an external firm
02
Independent Investigation
Within 30 Days
  • New leadership commissions a credible third-party investigation — former FBI director, judge, or prosecutor; no ties to existing leadership
  • Scope: abuse allegations, reporting channels, leadership decision-making, board oversight, NDA use, institutional culture
  • Findings committed to be made public in a written report, not a private memo — following the Penn State model
  • Survivor access with trauma-informed processes and clear guarantees participation won't limit legal rights
03
Victim-Centered Reform
Ongoing
  • New leadership issues a comprehensive statement acknowledging institutional failures, not merely Pete Newman's personal crimes
  • Settlement NDAs voluntarily released where legally permissible, consistent with the spirit of Trey's Law
  • Victim advisory council established with genuine authority
  • Child Protection Plan submitted for independent external audit and certification
  • Defamation posture against Ryan abandoned; legal resources redirected to victim support
Cost of Inaction

What Waiting Costs

  • → The defamation threat will catalyze, not suppress, coverage. Ryan has 5.7 million YouTube subscribers. Filing suit would create a years-long news cycle with discovery surfacing information Kanakuk cannot control.
  • → Discovery would be devastating. Ryan's attorney: "We welcome depositions of Kanakuk leadership about what they knew, when they knew it, and what they did or didn't do to protect children."
  • → Enrollment and donor relationships will erode. The SBC lost 1.5 million members. The BSA lost 60% of its membership. Kanakuk's premium market positioning makes it uniquely vulnerable to reputational damage.
  • → The NDA defense is collapsing in real time. Texas. Missouri. Five more states pending. The legal tools Kanakuk relied on to manage this crisis are being dismantled state by state.

"The question before the Board is not whether leadership transition will happen, but whether the Board will manage it proactively and with dignity — or whether external forces will impose it at far greater cost to the ministry, its staff, its families, and its mission."

Before the Call

If the investigation vindicates current leadership, the Board gains the strongest possible shield.
If it does not, new leadership becomes the clearest path to integrity and mission credibility.

Either way, the investigation is the right move.
The ask on this call
Kanakuk Ministries has nearly 100 years of positive impact. That legacy is worth protecting. Protecting it requires the courage to make difficult changes now, rather than allowing the crisis to deepen until those changes are forced by courts, regulators, or catastrophic enrollment declines.
